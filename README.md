# Sales-Strategy-Optimization

## 📌 Project Overview
This project involves a data-driven evaluation of three different sales methods (Email, Call, and Email + Call) to determine the most effective strategy for a new product launch. The goal was to identify which method yields the highest revenue while balancing the time cost for the sales team.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Tools
- **Language:** Python
- **Libraries:** Pandas (Data Cleaning & Manipulation), Matplotlib & Seaborn (Data Visualization).
- **Environment:** Jupyter Notebook / Google Colab.

## 🧹 Key Steps & Methodology
1. **Data Validation & Cleaning:** - Handled missing values in the `revenue` column.
   - Standardized categorical labels for sales methods.
   - Verified data integrity across customer IDs and visit counts.
2. **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):**
   - Analyzed the distribution of customers across different methods.
   - Visualized revenue spread using Box plots and Histograms.
   - Tracked revenue growth over time (weekly analysis).
3. **Metric Definition:**
   - Developed a **"Sales Efficiency"** metric (Revenue per Minute of Work) to account for the human effort required for each method.

## 💡 Key Findings
- **Customer Volume:** The 'Email' method reached the highest number of customers.
- **Revenue Performance:** The **'Email + Call'** method generated the highest average revenue (~$183) and showed consistent growth every week.
- **Efficiency:** While 'Email' is the fastest, 'Email + Call' offers the best balance of high-ticket sales and manageable time cost.

## 🚀 Final Recommendations
- **Primary Strategy:** Adopt the **'Email + Call'** method as the main sales approach due to its high growth potential and superior average revenue.
- **Secondary Strategy:** Use 'Email' as a low-cost supporting method for high-volume outreach.
